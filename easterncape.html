Eastern Cape

About this province

The Eastern Cape is one of South Africaâ€™s nine provinces, known for its rich history, diverse landscapes, and cultural significance. Its capital is Bhisho, while its largest city is Gqeberha (formerly Port Elizabeth).

The province is famous for its rugged coastline, including the Wild Coast, and its mountainous terrain, which includes parts of the Drakensberg.

Historically, the Eastern Cape was home to the Xhosa Kingdom, one of the most powerful African kingdoms, and later became a settlement area for British colonists in the 19th century.

It has produced many anti-apartheid leaders, including Nelson Mandela, Steve Biko, and Desmond Tutu.

Economically, the province relies on automotive manufacturing, agriculture, and tourism. It boasts two major industrial development zones, Coega and East London, which support exports. The Addo Elephant National Park and Mountain Zebra National Park are popular wildlife destinations.
