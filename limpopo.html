About this province

Limpopo is South Africaâ€™s northernmost province, named after the Limpopo River, which forms its western and northern borders. Its capital is Polokwane, a bustling city known for its economic and cultural significance.

  • Geography: The province boasts diverse landscapes, including savannahs, mountains, and bushveld, making it a prime location for wildlife conservation.
  • Culture & History: Limpopo is home to the Venda, Pedi, and Tsonga ethnic groups, each with rich traditions. The Mapungubwe National Park holds archaeological treasures from an ancient African kingdom.
  • Economy: The province thrives on mining, agriculture, and tourism. It has vast reserves of platinum, diamonds, and coal, and is a major producer of citrus fruits and avocados.
  • Wildlife & Nature: Limpopo hosts parts of the Kruger National Park, the Waterberg Biosphere Reserve, and the Magoebaskloof forests, offering breathtaking scenery and biodiversity.
  • Tourism: Popular attractions include the Baobab trees, hot springs of Bela Bela, and the Lake Fundudzi, which is steeped in Venda mythology.
